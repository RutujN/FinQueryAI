AAPL Q2 2026 Financial Performance & Analysis Report
Date: May 12, 2026
Prepared by: FinQuery Research Group

Overview:
Apple Inc. (NASDAQ: AAPL) announced its financial results for its fiscal 2026 second quarter ended March 28, 2026. The company posted a quarterly revenue of $98.4 billion, up 4.5% year-over-year (YoY), driven by robust demand for its new AI-integrated iPhone 17 series and an all-time record revenue in the Services division.

Key Financial Indicators:
- Revenue: $98.4 billion (vs. $94.2 billion in Q2 2025, +4.5% YoY)
- Net Income: $25.2 billion (vs. $23.6 billion in Q2 2025, +6.8% YoY)
- Diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS): $1.62 (up from $1.53 in Q2 2025)
- Gross Margin: 46.2% (driven by favorable product mix and Services growth)
- Cash and Cash Equivalents: $74.8 billion
- Total Debt: $102.5 billion

Segment Performance:
1. iPhone Segment:
   - Revenue: $48.2 billion, accounting for 49% of total revenue (up 2.1% YoY).
   - Growth was primarily driven by the iPhone 17 Pro and Pro Max models, which feature on-device "Apple Intelligence" neural cores. Average Selling Price (ASP) rose to $985.
2. Services Segment:
   - Revenue: $26.8 billion, representing 27.2% of total revenue (up 12.4% YoY).
   - This record high was driven by increases in active paid subscriptions across Apple Music, iCloud+, Apple Pay, and Apple TV+. Total paid subscriptions exceeded 1.15 billion.
3. Mac & iPad Segments:
   - Mac Revenue: $7.9 billion (+3.0% YoY), boosted by M5-powered MacBook Air models.
   - iPad Revenue: $5.6 billion (-1.5% YoY), reflecting longer upgrade cycles despite the launch of new OLED iPad Air variants.
4. Wearables, Home, and Accessories:
   - Revenue: $9.9 billion (+1.2% YoY). The Apple Watch Ultra 3 and upgraded AirPods Pro saw steady adoption, offset by flat Smart Home sales.

Strategic R&D & Capital Allocation:
- Apple spent $8.4 billion on Research & Development (R&D) this quarter, an increase of 14% YoY, heavily indexing on custom silicon, private cloud compute nodes, and next-generation robotics.
- The Board of Directors declared a cash dividend of $0.26 per share and authorized an additional $110 billion for share repurchases.

Outlook & Guidance for Q3 2026:
- Apple expects revenue growth to be similar to the March quarter (+4% to +5% range).
- Gross margin is expected to be between 45.5% and 46.5%.
- Operating expenses are estimated to be between $14.3 billion and $14.5 billion.
